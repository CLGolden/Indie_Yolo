Frequently Asked Questions

What will my website look like?

Based on the principles of BMSU. If you confuse, you lose. Since this is a beginners program, we assume you'll need help with colors and a logo. Text logo, favicon

Will I be able to update the website myself?

We will be building a static website. Occasional updates like hours, testimonials, etc.

There are many 'no-code' website solutions available now. Even so, they each have their own learning curve, and it's been my experience that busy business owners don't have the time or desire to learn them. So, they hire or assign someone to do it.

In addition, the 'no-code' website builders require you to use their hosting service and forbid you to take your work anywhere else. In other words, you are renting a website from them. After just the 2nd month of this program, you will own your website and all its content.

You'll need someone who knows HTML/CSS and other technical basics to make occasional updates to the site we're going to build. Many people want to learn how to code these days and I can help with that.

Once this program is well-established, I will be offering paid apprenticehips to beginners as 'Technical Assistants.' They will be recieve on-the-job-training to help graduates of this program make updates at an affordable price.

Why won't we be using Facebook or Instagram?

In this program, we will not be using any of the Facebook social media products. The most practical reason is that social media is for socializing.

Instead, we will be using Google My Business (GMB) and fundamental Search Engine Optimization (SEO) techniques. This approach reaches an audience who is more ready to buy your product or service.

What would this program cost on the open market?

The retail, for-profit cost of this program would be around $3000.

How much traffic will my website get?

More than zero... Cumulative improvement in organic search... Focus on 5-10 keywords... Strong foundation... Good setup for more elaborate marketing methods.

We will not accept a new business that directly competes with an existing program member.
